## Step 1: Understanding Word Embeddings (15 minutes)

Let's start with one of the most fundamental concepts in AI: how computers understand words.

### 🎯 Your Mission
Navigate to the **Word Embeddings Visualization** and complete these tasks:

#### Task 1.1: Basic Exploration (5 minutes)
1. **Start Simple**: Select the "animals" word set
2. **Observe**: Look at how words cluster together on the 2D plot
3. **Question**: Which animals are grouped together? Which are separated?

**💡 What to Notice**: Similar animals (like pets) should appear close together, while different types (pets vs. wild animals) might be separated.

#### Task 1.2: Similarity Search (5 minutes)
1. **Search**: Type "puppy" in the similarity search box
2. **Analyze**: Look at which existing words score highest similarity
3. **Experiment**: Try searching for "kitten" and compare results

**💡 Key Insight**: Words with similar meanings should have similar similarity scores, showing how AI captures semantic relationships mathematically.

#### Task 1.3: Parameter Exploration (5 minutes)
1. **Adjust**: Change the vector dimensions from 50 to 100, then to 200
2. **Observe**: How do the positions change? 
3. **Add Words**: Add your own words using the custom input: "puppy, kitten, bird, eagle"

**💡 Learning Moment**: Different dimension sizes affect how precisely meaning is captured - there's a balance between detail and computational efficiency.

### 🤔 Reflection Questions
- How might search engines use these embeddings to understand your queries?
- Why might "cat" and "dog" be closer than "cat" and "mathematics"?
- What happens to the visualization when you add very different words?

## Step 2: Discovering Attention (15 minutes)

Now that you understand how AI represents words, let's explore how AI decides what to focus on.

### 🎯 Your Mission
Navigate to the **Attention Mechanism Visualization** (when available) or continue with embedding concepts.

#### Task 2.1: Understanding Focus (5 minutes)
1. **Simple Sentence**: Start with "The cat sat on the mat"
2. **Attention Patterns**: Click on different words to see attention weights
3. **Relationships**: Notice which words "attend to" each other

**💡 What to Look For**: 
- Does "cat" pay attention to "sat"? (subject-verb relationship)
- How does "on" connect to "mat"? (prepositional relationship)

#### Task 2.2: Complex Sentences (5 minutes)
1. **Add Complexity**: Try "The brown cat that I saw yesterday sat on the soft mat"
2. **Long Dependencies**: See how "cat" relates to "sat" across more words
3. **Multiple Relationships**: Notice how "brown" connects to "cat" and "soft" to "mat"

**💡 Key Insight**: Attention allows AI to maintain relationships between words regardless of distance, solving the "memory problem" of earlier AI systems.

#### Task 2.3: Multi-Head Exploration (5 minutes)
1. **Different Heads**: Switch between different attention heads
2. **Specialized Patterns**: Notice how each head focuses on different types of relationships
3. **Combined Understanding**: See how multiple perspectives create richer understanding

**💡 Learning Moment**: Just like humans use different types of thinking (logical, emotional, creative), AI uses multiple attention heads to understand different aspects of language.

### 🤔 Reflection Questions
- How is attention similar to how you focus when reading?
- Why might multiple attention heads be better than just one?
- How could this help AI understand conversations or documents?

## Step 3: Connecting the Concepts (10 minutes)

Now let's see how embeddings and attention work together in real AI systems.

### 🎯 Your Mission
Connect what you've learned by exploring how these concepts build on each other.

#### Task 3.1: The Full Picture (3 minutes)
1. **Embeddings First**: Remember how words become numbers (vectors)
2. **Attention Second**: These vectors are used in attention to find relationships
3. **Combined Power**: Attention operates on embeddings to understand meaning

**💡 Connection**: Embeddings give words mathematical meaning, attention decides which meanings to focus on.

#### Task 3.2: Real-World Thinking (4 minutes)
Think about these AI applications and identify where embeddings and attention are used:

1. **Google Search**: How does it understand your query and find relevant results?
   - *Embeddings*: Convert your search terms to vectors
   - *Attention*: Focus on the most relevant parts of web pages

2. **ChatGPT Conversations**: How does it maintain context in long conversations?
   - *Embeddings*: Represent each part of the conversation
   - *Attention*: Remember and connect to relevant earlier parts

3. **Language Translation**: How does Google Translate maintain meaning across languages?
   - *Embeddings*: Represent meaning independent of specific language
   - *Attention*: Align related concepts between languages

#### Task 3.3: Looking Ahead (3 minutes)
Preview what's coming next in your learning journey:
- **Transformers**: The architecture that combines embeddings and attention
- **Language Models**: How these pieces create AI that can generate text
- **Large Language Models**: How scaling up creates surprising capabilities

### 🤔 Integration Questions
- How do embeddings and attention solve different but related problems?
- Why might you need both concepts for effective AI?
- What other areas might benefit from these ideas?
